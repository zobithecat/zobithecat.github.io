Current R&D focus at VENDIT

Disaster communications for deeply enclosed spaces. Underground utility tunnels, construction sites, hospitality facilities --- the kind of environments where the conventional wireless coverage collapses on the first concrete wall, and where in every recent Korean disaster (소공동 화재, 광명 붕괴, 포항 침수, 이천 물류센터 화재) the common thread has been the same: communication lost, location unknown, evacuation guidance absent. We are building a self-healing dual-band wireless mesh (BLE 2.4 GHz + LoRa 920 MHz) that survives infrastructure loss, paired with an integrated smart-mask form factor (toxic-gas sensing, sub-5 m indoor positioning, on-device AI evacuation guidance). Government-funded R&D, 2026.06 onward, with Prof. Mooyoung Yoo at Daejin University Department of Architectural Engineering --- a reunion of the 2017--2019 "safety dream team" whose MSS Yeonguhmaul work (programme C0505520) produced the patents KR 10-2034554 and KR 10-2033118 that anchor this next-generation effort.

Selected open-source projects

airspy-mini-dmb 2026

End-to-end open-source software receiver for Korean Terrestrial DMB on low-cost SDR hardware.

Pure-Python pipeline: ETI parsing → Fast Information Channel decode → Reed-Solomon (204, 188) + Forney convolutional deinterleaver outer FEC → MPEG-4 SL packet demux → H.264 elementary stream reconstruction → ffmpeg decode. Verified on a 30 MB on-air capture of YTN DMB (channel K8B, 183.008 MHz) with an Airspy Mini and an indoor antenna: 87.3 % of Reed-Solomon blocks decode cleanly and ffmpeg renders QVGA H.264 frames containing recognisable Korean broadcast content.

Papers (preprint + Korean companion) are in the paper/ directory.

Stack: Python · C++ · LaTeX · Airspy Mini SDR · ETI(NI) · ETSI EN 300 401 · ETSI TS 102 427/428 · TTAK.KO-07.0026 · ISO/IEC 14496-1
